Model assumptions & caveats

What this is: a simplified national-level digital twin. One year (8 760 h) of synthetic demand, wind and solar profiles — calibrated to Eskom's weekly system status reports (June–July 2026): winter evening peaks ≈ 26–27.3 GW, fleet EAF ≈ 64 %, OCGT load factor ≈ 1.3 %, 8.3 GW of embedded rooftop PV, and the post-2023 structural demand decline. Dispatch is by merit order: rooftop PV nets off demand → wind / utility PV / CSP → nuclear, hydro, Cahora Bassa imports → coal (× EAF) → pumped storage & batteries → gas CCGT → diesel OCGT → unserved energy. One stage of load shedding ≈ 1 000 MW of unserved demand. Storage recharges from surplus renewables and off-peak coal headroom.

Outage risk: coal availability follows a mean-corrected daily AR(1) process around the EAF slider, with volatility calibrated to the multi-week swings in Eskom's reported unplanned outages. The risk panel dispatches 60 such synthetic years and reports load shedding as probabilities. Weather and demand noise are held fixed across runs, so the bands reflect outage risk only — real years also vary in weather.

What it is not: transmission is schematic only (no network constraints), no unit commitment or operating reserves. For decision-grade work, use PyPSA-RSA — this app is the intuition layer in front of it.

Built as a demonstrator in the spirit of PyPSA-CA. Fleet capacities and profiles approximate the 2026 South African system (calibrated to Eskom weekly system status reports, June–July 2026). All outputs are indicative — this is an educational scenario explorer, not planning or investment advice.
